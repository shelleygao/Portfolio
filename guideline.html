The kitchen was the basic version of project F, which provided complete app design for dozens of Banks. The purpose of Kitchen specification lied in user-centered design, comprehensive consideration of all kinds of use conditions, to ensure the same user experience between different types of equipment and modules.

Why did we design kitchen specifications?
We had to provide solutions for dozens of banks at the same time, we needed:
1. Agile development mode: in the process of communication with clients, we could immediately demonstrate the mature high-fidelity prototype, quickly complete the adjustment based on the basic version according to the clients' requirements.
2. Detailed division of labor: fixed the designers and developers of each module, so that everyone was very familiar with the module he/she was responsible for, so as to facilitate the continuous optimization of user experience in future iterations.

Therefore, in the context of multi-person collaborative design, it was very necessary to design a set of norms to guide all designers to achieve the consistency of visual interaction. Similarly, it was also important to set the boundary and scope of design, just like everyone was running on the same road, but allowing a certain range of deviation.

How did we do?
In the early stage, a designer was assigned to do preliminary research and started to write the guideline. In the process of writing, relevant schemes of team members should be continuously summarized, and the team should discuss which elements could be put into the guideline and which parts needed to be modified. Finally, the guideline was applied to new projects and was constantly validated and iterated.

What was the difficulty？
1. There were too many cases and clients' demands, and the guideline needed to be adjusted constantly for the situations that had not been considered before.
2. Complex third-party design schemes couldn't achieve perfect integration in a short time.
3. Although the mock-up was designed according to the guideline, the fit between development and design was not very high, which was mainly caused by the more detailed division of labor between developers than designers and the communication problems between the two cities.

What problem did we solve?
1. The theme and layout of the app main page were independent of the guideline and completely customized the design for customers. Special persons were assigned to this part, but it was still obeyed the grid system. This would meet the personalized needs of clients.
2. After the kitchen version was developed, this operable high fidelity prototype could help negotiate and communicate with our bank clients in a timely manner. All F projects would be developed on the basis of the kitchen version, which greatly saved labor cost and improved development efficiency. In the meantime, the kitchen kept gathering cases and refining common templates and updating guideline.
3. Sharing guideline with developers and working with them on frequent travel to improve overall visual consistency and user experience.
